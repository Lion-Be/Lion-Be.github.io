--- layout: archive permalink: /year-archive/ title: "Blog posts" author_profile: true redirect_from: - /wordpress/blog-posts/ --- Algorithmic bias is an inherent challenge in machine learning models, particularly in high-stakes applications like credit approval, where fairness is critical. In this blog post, I identify four key sources of bias in AI models: - Bias in Human Decisions: Models trained on biased historical data may replicate discriminatory patterns. - Proxy Attributes: Even without explicit sensitive data, indirect variables like geographic location can encode sensitive attributes, perpetuating bias. - Sample Selection Bias: Disparities in data representation can skew outcomes, favoring majority groups. - Algorithmic Objectives: Optimization processes focusing on majority group accuracy may lead to poor performance for minorities.
